Waste in Numbers

In the US alone, consumers dispose of some 3.2 million tons of e-waste annually; more than 80% ends up in the trash. Globally, it is estimated that only 13% of e-waste undergoes some form of recycling; an overwhelming proportion of e-waste is dumped in the developing world. The dumping of e-waste represents not only an environmental disaster, but also the loss of millions of tons of valuable resources.
